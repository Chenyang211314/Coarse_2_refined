The Right Semantics at the Right Time: Staged Semantics for Generative Trajectories

A coarse-to-refined inference strategy that uses coarse semantics during high-noise formation and the full prompt during low-noise refinement.

Method Overview

The method separates early coarse-grained semantic formation from later fine-grained constraint realization, while adapting the transition point and sampling budget to the prompt gap.

Stage 1: coarse formation

The simplified prompt guides primary objects, spatial layout, and global motion in the high-noise region.

Stage 2: detail refinement

The full prompt guides local events, appearance attributes, and background details in the low-noise region.

Subject objects

02

Spatial layout

Scene configuration is formed before local refinement.

Prompt: A river floods over its banks beside a rural road, carrying muddy water and grass clumps past fence posts and a half-submerged mailbox.
03

Global motion

Motion trajectory is guided by coarse semantics in early denoising.

Prompt: A man eats noodles from a bowl, lifting strands with chopsticks while broth and vegetables are visible near his hands.
04

Local events

Later denoising allocates capacity to local interactions and events.

Prompt: White milk swirls into black coffee inside a clear cup, forming curved patterns that fade into light brown liquid.
05

Appearance attributes

Fine visual attributes are refined after global structure is established.

Prompt: A lighthouse stands on the ocean shore, with a tall white tower, rocky coast, waves breaking below, and open sea stretching behind it.
06

Background details

Background details are improved without destabilizing the main layout.

Prompt: An orange clownfish weaves through anemone in a coral lagoon, where soft anemone tentacles sway around its body, and purple coral heads sit nearby.
